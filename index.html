🚗 General Road Rules
Core rules that apply to every driver on every road.
Driving Side: Rwanda drives on the RIGHT side of the road. The steering wheel is on the LEFT side of the vehicle. This differs from Kenya and Uganda, which drive on the left.
↔️ Overtaking (Passing) ▾
  • Always overtake on the LEFT — pass on the left side of the vehicle ahead (into the oncoming lane).
  • Overtaking on the right (undertaking) is prohibited — the driver cannot see you from their blind side.
  • Overtaking is forbidden near bridges with narrow roadways, near pedestrian crossings, and on dangerous road sections.
  • Solid white centre line = no overtaking. Broken white centre line = overtaking permitted when safe.
🔄 Roundabouts ▾
  • Vehicles already inside the roundabout have right of way. Entering vehicles must yield.
  • Yield to traffic coming from the right when entering.
  • Signal right before exiting. Do not change lanes inside unless markings allow it.
💺 Seat Belts & Child Safety ▾
  • Mandatory for all passengers — front and rear seats.
  • Fine for violation: RWF 10,000 per unbelted passenger.
  • Children under 3 must be in an approved child restraint (car seat).
  • Children under 12 cannot sit in the front seat — even when no other seats are available.
🚶 Pedestrians ▾
  • Pedestrians have right of way at zebra crossings. Drivers must stop completely.
  • When passing a pedestrian near an obstacle, if a 1 m free space cannot be maintained, maximum speed is 20 km/h.
  • Pavements and shoulders are reserved for pedestrians when no special regulation is posted.
  • Parking is forbidden where pedestrians must use the roadway to pass an obstacle.
📱 Mobile Phones & Distractions ▾
  • Using a mobile phone while driving is prohibited unless using a hands-free device.
  • Includes talking, texting, or browsing — any active use of the device.
  • Pull over to a safe location before making a call.
🍺 Alcohol & Drugs ▾
  • Legal blood alcohol concentration (BAC) limit: 0.08%.
  • Zero-tolerance is commonly applied to new and professional drivers.
  • Penalties: heavy fines, licence suspension, or imprisonment.
  • Random breathalyser checkpoints are common, especially at night and on weekends.
🚨 Emergency Vehicles ▾
  • Pull to the right and stop when an emergency vehicle approaches with sirens and lights.
  • Blue flashing lights visible in all directions = priority vehicle.
  • Do not follow closely behind emergency vehicles in motion.
  • Emergency number: 112.
🚀 Speed Limits
Vehicle-type limits override zone limits — a bus on a highway is still limited to 60 km/h.
Speeding fine: RWF 50,000. Repeat offence within the same period = fine × 9 (RWF 450,000). Speed cameras and police radar guns are routinely deployed in Kigali and on national roads.

⚖️ Fines & Enforcement
Rwanda enforces traffic laws strictly. Cameras, radar, and checkpoints are routine.
